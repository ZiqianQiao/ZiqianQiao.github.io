😊 About Me

I am Ziqian Qiao, a master's student at Beijing University of Technology. My research focuses on medical artificial intelligence, biomedical data mining, interpretable clinical decision support, and trustworthy medication recommendation.

My recent work centers on hypertension risk prediction, knowledge-augmented medication recommendation, contraindication-aware modeling, and interpretable deep-to-shallow distillation for transparent clinical decision paths. I am particularly interested in combining domain knowledge, generative modeling, and explainable AI to build reliable medical decision systems.

A Hypertension Early Warning Model Combining Generative Adversarial Networks and Long Short-Term Memory Neural Networks

S. Lin, Z. Qiao, J. Chen, Z. Huang

WISE 2024, 2025 CCF-C

Paper

Introduction: This paper proposes a hypertension early warning framework for small-sample medical scenarios. It combines generative adversarial networks with long short-term memory modeling to augment limited clinical data and improve temporal risk prediction. The method is designed to enhance early identification of hypertension risk under constrained and imbalanced EHR conditions.

TSAC-DRM: A Drug Recommendation Model Based on Two-Stage Attention Mechanism and Constraint Learning

S. Lin, Z. Qiao, J. Chen

IEEE/WIC International Conference on Web Intelligence and Intelligent Agent Technology (WI-IAT 2024), 2024 CCF-C

Paper

Introduction: This work introduces a drug recommendation framework based on a two-stage attention mechanism and constraint learning. It models longitudinal patient history more effectively, while explicitly considering medication safety constraints. The framework aims to improve the accuracy, temporal modeling capacity, and clinical rationality of recommendation results.

HRP-OG: Online Learning with Generative Feature Replay for Hypertension Risk Prediction in a Nonstationary Environment

S. Lin, H. Yan, S. Zhou, Z. Qiao, J. Chen

Sensors, 2024 SCI · Q2 · IF 3.5

Paper

Introduction: This paper studies hypertension risk prediction in nonstationary clinical environments. It proposes an online learning strategy with generative feature replay to address data drift, class imbalance, and sparse longitudinal visits. The framework improves robustness and adaptability for real-world EHR-based risk prediction tasks.

KATMed: A Knowledge-Augmented Transformer Model for Contraindication-Aware Medication Recommendation in Comorbidities

Z. Qiao, S. Lin, J. Fan, J. Chen, Z. Tang, Z. Zhang

Journal of Biomedical Informatics, 2025/2026 SCI · Q2 · IF 4.5

Paper

Introduction: KATMed is a knowledge-augmented transformer model for contraindication-aware medication recommendation in comorbidity scenarios. It integrates positive medical knowledge with negative safety constraints to jointly optimize recommendation accuracy and prescription safety. The model is designed to provide more reliable support for complex clinical decision-making.

DSS-D: A Deep-to-Shallow Synergistic Distillation Framework for Interpretable Deep Medication Recommendation Modeling

Z. Qiao, S. Lin, J. Chen

Manuscript in progress Interpretable AI

Paper (Coming Soon)

Introduction: DSS-D is an interpretable deep-to-shallow synergistic distillation framework for medication recommendation. It distills a deep neural recommender into a shallow multi-label decision-tree structure with symbolic decision paths, while introducing adaptive clinical co-pattern encoding. The framework aims to enhance interpretability without discarding the predictive strength of deep models.
